## Philosophy

### The Story Arc
A great pitch deck follows a narrative:
1. **Hook**: Capture attention with the problem
2. **Build**: Show the opportunity and your solution
3. **Prove**: Demonstrate traction and team capability
4. **Ask**: Clear funding request and use

### Key Principles
- **10-15 slides maximum** (12 is the sweet spot)
- **One idea per slide**
- **Visual over text** — minimize bullet points
- **Numbers speak louder** than adjectives
- **Honesty wins** — sophisticated investors see through spin

## Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Title
- Company name and tagline
- Your name and contact
- Date and version

### Slide 2: Problem (The Hook)
**Goal**: Make the audience feel the pain.

- Specific, relatable problem statement
- Quantify the pain (cost, time, frustration)
- Show who has this problem and how many
- Why existing solutions fail

**Best format**: Customer quote or real scenario + supporting data.

### Slide 3: Solution
**Goal**: The "aha" moment.

- Clear, simple explanation (no jargon)
- Show, do not tell — screenshot, demo, visual
- Core value proposition in one sentence
- Direct link to the problem from Slide 2

### Slide 4: Why Now
**Goal**: Create urgency.

- Market timing factors
- Technology enablers
- Regulatory changes
- Behavioral shifts
- Why this could not have worked 5 years ago

### Slide 5: Market Opportunity
**Goal**: Show the prize is worth chasing.

- TAM/SAM/SOM with clear methodology
- Growth rate and direction
- Bottom-up sizing is more credible than top-down alone
- Preempt the obvious "but what about..." objections

### Slide 6: Business Model
**Goal**: Show how you make money.

- Revenue streams
- Pricing model and rationale
- Unit economics (CAC, LTV, margins)
- Path to profitability

### Slide 7: Traction
**Goal**: Prove it is working.

- Key metrics: revenue, users, growth rate
- Customer logos (with permission)
- Month-over-month progression
- Highlight inflection points

**No traction yet?** Show:
- Letters of intent, pilot agreements
- Waitlist numbers
- Advisor commitments
- Early user feedback or survey data

### Slide 8: Product
**Goal**: Make it tangible.

- Screenshots or demo
- Key features (max 3-4, not a feature list)
- Technology differentiation (if relevant)
- Roadmap highlights (next 12 months only)

### Slide 9: Competition
**Goal**: Show you understand the landscape.

- 2x2 competitive matrix (position yourself in the top-right)
- Honest about competitors' strengths
- Clear, specific differentiation
- Why you win

**Never say "we have no competition."** It signals naivety, not opportunity.

### Slide 10: Go-to-Market
**Goal**: Show you can sell.

- Customer acquisition strategy
- Sales motion (self-serve, inside sales, enterprise)
- Key channels
- Early wins and learnings

### Slide 11: Team
**Goal**: Build confidence in execution.

- Founders with relevant experience highlighted
- Key hires and advisors
- Why this team for this problem
- Gaps acknowledged with plan to fill them

Include photos, previous company logos, relevant credentials.

### Slide 12: Financials
**Goal**: Show path to returns.

- 3-year projection (Revenue, Gross Margin, EBITDA)
- Key assumptions stated
- Path to profitability or next milestone
- Keep high-level — details in appendix

### Slide 13: The Ask
**Goal**: Clear call to action.

- Amount raising
- Use of funds (pie chart or simple table)
- Key milestones this funding enables
- Current round details (lead investor, terms if set)

### Slide 14: Closing
- Company name and tagline
- Contact information

### Appendix Slides (as needed)
- Detailed financial model
- Full competitive analysis
- Technical architecture
- Customer case studies
- Extended team bios
- Patent/IP summary
- Market research sources

## Visual Design Guidelines

### Typography
- Sans-serif fonts (clean, modern feel)
- Max 2 font sizes per slide
- Minimum 24pt for body text
- Titles readable from the back of a room

### Color
- 2-3 colors maximum
- High contrast for readability
- Consistent with brand
- Charts should be instantly readable

### Layout
- Generous white space
- Left-to-right reading flow
- One focal point per slide
- Consistent margins and alignment

### Data Visualization
- Simple chart types (bar, line, pie)
- Label data points directly on the chart
- Remove chart junk (unnecessary gridlines, redundant legends)
- Make the takeaway obvious — do not make the audience work

## Delivery Tips

### Know Your Numbers
- Memorize key metrics cold
- Know customer counts, growth rates, unit economics
- Have financial detail ready for Q&A

### Anticipate These Questions
- Why now?
- Why you? (team-market fit)
- What is the moat?
- How do you acquire customers profitably?
- What keeps you up at night?
- Why this valuation?
- What happens if [key assumption] is wrong?

### Demo Readiness
- Have backup screenshots if live demo fails
- Know the happy path
- Do not show unfinished features or known bugs

## Output Format

Deliver the pitch deck as:
1. **Slide-by-slide content** — What each slide says (copy + data)
2. **Key messages** — What each slide must communicate (single sentence)
3. **Suggested visuals** — Charts, screenshots, images needed
4. **Talking points** — What to say on each slide (not on the slide itself)

If the user wants a file, suggest exporting via a document generation tool or provide content ready for paste into presentation software.

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Tells a clear story from problem to solution to ask
- [ ] Every claim backed by data
- [ ] Competitive analysis is honest
- [ ] Financials are defensible under questioning
- [ ] Clear ask with specific use of funds
- [ ] Team slide inspires confidence
- [ ] Visual design is clean and consistent
- [ ] Under 15 slides (excluding appendix)
- [ ] No slide has more than 6 lines of text
