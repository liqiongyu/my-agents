# Research Frameworks Reference

Select the appropriate framework based on the nature of the research topic. Multiple frameworks may be combined for comprehensive analysis.

## PESTEL Analysis

Best for: market/industry research, strategic analysis, macro-environment assessment.

| Factor | Key Questions |
|--------|--------------|
| **Political** | Government policy, regulation, political stability, trade policy, tax policy |
| **Economic** | GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, exchange rates, unemployment, consumer spending |
| **Social** | Demographics, cultural trends, consumer behavior, education, health consciousness |
| **Technological** | Innovation pace, automation, R&D activity, technology adoption, digital infrastructure |
| **Environmental** | Sustainability, climate impact, resource scarcity, environmental regulations, carbon footprint |
| **Legal** | Employment law, consumer protection, data privacy, IP rights, compliance requirements |

### How to Apply

1. Assign one or more sub-questions to each PESTEL factor relevant to the topic
2. Search for information specifically about each factor's impact on the topic
3. Rate each factor's influence (High / Medium / Low) with supporting evidence
4. Identify interactions between factors (e.g., political regulation driving technological change)

### Example: PESTEL for "Electric Vehicle Market in Europe"

- **Political**: EU emissions targets, EV subsidies, ban on ICE vehicles by 2035
- **Economic**: Battery cost trends, charging infrastructure investment, consumer purchasing power
- **Social**: Green consumer sentiment, range anxiety perception, urbanization trends
- **Technological**: Battery energy density improvements, charging speed, autonomous driving integration
- **Environmental**: Lifecycle carbon analysis, battery recycling challenges, mining impact
- **Legal**: CO2 emission standards, battery passport regulation, right-to-repair directives

## 5W1H Framework

Best for: general-purpose topic exploration, investigative research, comprehensive overviews.

| Dimension | Questions |
|-----------|----------|
| **What** | Definition, scope, components, types, characteristics |
| **Why** | Causes, motivations, rationale, drivers, significance |
| **Who** | Stakeholders, actors, affected parties, key players, decision-makers |
| **When** | Timeline, milestones, history, key dates, phases of development |
| **Where** | Geography, contexts, applications, markets, deployment environments |
| **How** | Mechanisms, processes, methods, implementation, technical details |

### How to Apply

1. Map each sub-question to one or more 5W1H dimensions
2. Ensure the research plan covers at least 4 of the 6 dimensions
3. Use gaps in the 5W1H grid to identify missing sub-questions
4. Structure the final report sections around the most relevant dimensions

### Example: 5W1H for "CRISPR Gene Editing"

- **What**: Mechanism of CRISPR-Cas9, types of edits (knockout, knock-in, base editing)
- **Why**: Precision over older methods, potential to cure genetic diseases, agricultural applications
- **Who**: Key researchers (Doudna, Charpentier), companies (Editas, CRISPR Therapeutics), patients
- **When**: Discovery timeline (2012–present), FDA approval milestones, clinical trial phases
- **Where**: Research labs, clinical settings, agricultural applications, regulatory environments by country
- **How**: Guide RNA design, delivery mechanisms, off-target detection, clinical protocols

## Argument Mapping

Best for: controversial topics, policy debates, technology evaluations with trade-offs, "should we" questions.

### Structure

```
Thesis: [Main claim or position]
│
├── Supporting Argument 1
│   ├── Evidence A [source]
│   ├── Evidence B [source]
│   └── Strength: Strong / Moderate / Weak
│
├── Supporting Argument 2
│   ├── Evidence C [source]
│   └── Strength: Strong / Moderate / Weak
│
├── Counter-argument 1
│   ├── Evidence D [source]
│   ├── Rebuttal: [response to counter-argument]
│   └── Strength: Strong / Moderate / Weak
│
└── Counter-argument 2
    ├── Evidence E [source]
    ├── Rebuttal: [response to counter-argument]
    └── Strength: Strong / Moderate / Weak
```

### How to Apply

1. Identify the central thesis or question
2. Search specifically for supporting evidence and arguments
3. Search specifically for counter-arguments and criticisms (do not skip this)
4. Rate the strength of each argument based on evidence quality
5. Identify rebuttals to counter-arguments
6. Present a balanced assessment noting where consensus exists and where genuine debate remains

### Example: Argument Mapping for "Should companies adopt a 4-day work week?"

```
Thesis: Companies should adopt a 4-day work week

├── Supporting: Productivity remains same or increases
│   ├── Microsoft Japan trial: 40% productivity increase [1]
│   ├── UK 4-day week pilot: 92% of companies continued [2]
│   └── Strength: Strong (multiple large-scale trials)
│
├── Supporting: Employee wellbeing and retention improve
│   ├── Reduced burnout rates by 71% in trials [2]
│   ├── 57% reduction in staff leaving [3]
│   └── Strength: Strong (consistent across studies)
│
├── Counter: Not suitable for all industries
│   ├── Healthcare, retail, manufacturing require continuous coverage [4]
│   ├── Rebuttal: Staggered schedules can maintain coverage
│   └── Strength: Moderate (valid but mitigable)
│
└── Counter: Revenue impact concerns
    ├── Some companies reported client-facing challenges [5]
    ├── Rebuttal: Async communication tools reduce real-time dependency
    └── Strength: Weak (limited evidence of revenue loss)
```

## Choosing the Right Framework

| Research Topic Type | Recommended Framework |
|---|---|
| Market analysis, industry outlook | PESTEL |
| General knowledge topic, "explain X" | 5W1H |
| Controversial or policy question | Argument Mapping |
| Technology evaluation / comparison | 5W1H + Argument Mapping |
| Strategic decision support | PESTEL + Argument Mapping |
| Investigative / "what happened" | 5W1H |

For complex research, combine frameworks: use 5W1H to structure the exploration, PESTEL to analyze the macro environment, and Argument Mapping to evaluate competing positions.
